SPEAKING TOPICS

Most organizations have long been on the web and have (mostly) figured out social media. But are you getting the most from your investment? And what is next - what is the "could-be?" Finally, how to give everyone a common vocabulary - and the motivation - to take advantage of what is coming down the pike.

Randall Craig helps organizations - and individuals - land their digital strategy airplane. He shares practical, been-there-before advice from his 23 years of Web and social media experience as a consultant, senior executive, entrepreneur, best-selling author (and Black Belt in karate). He does this for 12-person teams, 800-person webcasts, and at conferences with over 1500 people in the audience.

His most requested presentations:

  • Social Media Without Wasting Your Time or Killing Your Brand

    Strategy, productivity, and risk for attendees and their organizations

    It's all the rage to talk about LinkedIn, Facebook, and the 500+ other social networks that exist. But how can social media be used to leverage your organization's strategy - without wasting your time? How can you avoid the "dark side" and protect against identity theft, brand hijacking and other risks? And how do you measure results?

    Details

  • New Old Thinking on Thought Leadership: Inbound Marketing, Content Strategy, and Building Competitive Advantage

    Growing reputation and influence

    While most professionals work hard at becoming trusted advisors, becoming a "thought leader" is far more difficult - and in today's world, more strategic than ever. Based on the research in his books, Randall will share some of the newest thinking on how to use content, social media, and marketing automation to increase influence - and build sustainable competitive advantage. (And how to do this without wasting your time or blowing your budget.)

    Details

  • Digital Growth Hacking for Sales Professionals

    Strategy and practical tips to build an effective sales funnel

    Are you dubious about the ROI of social media for sales? If so, it’s likely because your efforts have been focused on marketing, not business development. Many sales professionals already have social media profiles, but identifying, approaching and then building relationships with prospective clients in social media almost seems "unprofessional". It isn't, if done strategically.

    Details

  • Digital Transformation: From Websites to Social to WTF

    Building capacity in the age of an always-changing world

    From iBeacons to social-CRM integration to machine learning, new technologies are changing the competitive landscape. At the same time, pressure to cut costs, deliver greater value, and be more responsive are forcing organizations - and individuals - to work far differently than before. In this presentation, Randall will describe these trends and futures, and how to retool to differentiate, connect, and engage.

    Details
