MORE ABOUT RANDALL

Randall Craig is a digital visionary with an obsession for creating order out of chaos.

Fresh from a 'big-four' consulting firm in the early 1990s, he built (and executed) the web strategies for major market newspapers, international financial institutions, professional service firms, associations, and over 100+ others.

Randall is a serial entrepreneur, leads the digital consulting firm
108 ideaspace, and continues to carve a path as a digital innovator. He shares his unique perspective – and questions conventional wisdom - through his seven books (one a best seller), 600+ articles, keynote speeches, workshops, and advisory relationships.

Randall blogs for The Huffington Post, hosts Professionally Speaking TV,
and frequently appears as an expert on radio, TV, and newspapers. For the last decade, he has served at the board level for several national organizations, and has earned a CFA, MBA, CMC, CSP. And a Black Belt in karate.

Avoiding Tech Project Failures June 10, 2016

Has your organization invested in a “game-changing” software project, only to discover that the promised benefits never really materialize? Or that the implementation was so flawed that the system is regarded as a financial and operating disaster?

Sadly, this happens far more than it should… but must this always be the case? Here are five key reasons for technology project failures – and how to avoid them.

1) No detailed Blueprint: A Blueprint is the planning engagement that maps your processes, data, and systems, then determines how a particular type of software will be used to achieve specific business goals. It often will include three other elements:

  • An early recommendation of specific software,
  • A high level technical architecture,
  • A listing of recommended business process changes. [More]
