SPEAKING TOPICS Social Media Without Wasting Your Time or Killing Your Brand Strategy, productivity, and risk for attendees and their organizations

It's all the rage to talk about LinkedIn, Facebook, and the 500+ other social networks that exist. But how can social media be used to leverage your organization's strategy - without wasting your time? How can you avoid the "dark side" and protect against identity theft, brand hijacking and other risks? And how do you measure results? In this fast-paced presentation, Randall Craig provides practical insight into these and other timely issues.

Every presentation is completely customized for the audience, and can be delivered as a keynote, workshop, webcast, or master class.

  • For Corporate Audiences:
    Seven social media strategies to grow your business – and avoid the risks. (Business development, recruiting, internal collaboration, risks and policy, and governance.)
  • For Associations and Not-for-profits:
    Harnessing the power of social media to improve member engagement, development and marketing. (Stakeholder engagement, fundraising, online PR, risks/policy, governance, and event marketing.)
  • For Professional Service Firms:
    Strategies and policies to implement a successful social media initiative – at the firm and individual levels. (Profile building, business development, prospect due diligence, recruiting, and risk.)
  • For Entrepreneurs/Experts/Consultants:
    How to translate personal profile to business growth online. (Profile building, thought leadership, business development, prospect due diligence, and risk.)
  • For Marketing Professionals:
    (Campaign development, online PR, integration with traditional marketing, SEO, Web integration, and marketing automation.)
  • For the Job Seeker:
    Advanced strategies for a social media job search. (Reactive and proactive social media; avoiding disqualification, advanced LinkedIn strategies, and avoiding identity theft.)

DELIVERY OPTIONS

  • Interactive worksop (2-4 hours)

  • VALUE BEYOND THE EVENT

  • BEFORE

    • A detailed questionnaire about your meeting objectives and audience
    • 2-4 interviews, to understand your priorities and audience issues.
    • Customized and updated presentation.
    • A movie-trailer promo video to help drive registration.
    • (Yes, Randall does rehearse.)

  • ON-SITE

    • Early arrival to meet attendees, listen to other speakers, and weave this intelligence into the presentation. Early arrival also means early sound checks.

    • Additional learning materials are optionally available (e.g. books).

  • AFTER
